Summer Swim

Red ants,
by the hundreds,
converge,
naked running feet

down the path,
wet slippery clay smashing
against tree roots
exposed

through the woods,
down the ravine
they scurry and slide, at last
rewarded by the cool clear current.

Laughing, splashing bodies, faces
weave amongst floating leaves
force through the water,
for the effortless gliding ride below the falls.

Toes touching the soft hidden sandy bottom,
sounds of joy
echo into the woods
reaching for the tree tops.

Red ants,
by the hundreds,
rest on the shore
waiting,

naked running feet
returning.

 

Solstice of the Heart

"Want to dance?" he asked
eyes of melting velvet, the mouth of a charmer
a roomful of perfumed smiles flashed
-- breath stilled by emotion
my pounding heart secretly chosen.

French bread, Pate and Chablis Blanc
soft summer waters, a lake of passion wine
naked caresses in transparent dark
our souls entwine
evening boaters of the park, gliding by.

In melodic romance, with Shelley and Wagner, enraptured
we dance and dance . . .
the hearth of an ember fire, a dozen summers captured
through the glimmer of a spider web
rainbow dust of time and fate.

The moonbeams still kiss the sea
endlessly, My Love
yet, I lay in the rose-scented bed, waiting for Thee

But, what is all this love worth
if Thou not come back to me.

 

Some thoughts of love... and some other thoughts, too

I want to melt
into the brown bottomless
depths, the love in a puppy's eyes
and stay there
Why should I leave
I need nothing else.

   -   -   -

Almost always there is somebody
who loves what you don't
black widows and roaches
thousand-year-old guano piles
even sardine pizza
have their admirers
dandruff on the shoulder
best against black
those little skin mites
must love that
and THAT is a good thing.

   -   -   -

If our love
was as deep as the ocean
I would want you to drink it empty
so I could see how deep it truly was
but then -
we would have to say
our love was as deep as
the pot you would need to piss in
Not very romantic, truly not.

   -   -   -

My lover has one eye made of blue lapis
the other of green jade
I cannot decide
which I like best
If the moon was made of jade
then perhaps that
but with my luck
the entire sky
would then be of blue lapis.

   -   -   -

Can we make midsummer magic out of words
or do we actually need some herbs?

 

If Only

We all pray at one time or another, my friend
If only I could do it, decide it, say or write it again
I'd be stronger and smarter
Kinder or harder
Mount Everest I'd climb
The brightest diamond I would find
   For you, for you
   For you, ourselves and others,
   we promise that,
   we promise that

Not all those times for me can I remember
Just the ones holding inner pain or anger
However small,
however small
Those kept locked away inside my heart waiting
For the chance to do it, decide it, say or write it again

One day a river forms as it collects from me
The secret tears that no one else can see
These drops of heartache in my soul, oh please
Please, hurry, take them out to the deepest sea
   For you, for you
   For you, myself and others,
   I promise that,
   I promise that

Just one blade of grass can force its way through the impasse
However small, however small

If only wishes had feathered wings
We could fly high up into the forever sky
And down would fall all our self-built dams
And the water would rush through our minds and hearts
And calm our bodies' secret places
And we would feel the smiles on our faces
And we would feel the smiles on our faces
We would feel the smiles on our faces
   For you, for you
   For you, myself and others,
   I promise that,
   I promise that

Just one blade of grass can force its way through the impasse
However small, however small
However small, however small
However small

And we would feel the smiles on our faces

 

Sirkka Shayan, 1994, (won second prize in a National Poetry competition)
